About Me

Hi, this is Ze Zhang (张泽). Welcome to my personal website!
I am a Ph.D. candidate in Management and Strategy at the University of Hong Kong. My research interests include nonmarket strategy, informal institutions, and emerging technologies. In the area of nonmarket strategy, I explore how firms strategically respond to institutional pressures regarding their sustainability efforts. I am particularly interested in how stakeholders and shareholders — empowered by informal institutions — shape firms' corporate governance and ESG outcomes. In my recent working projects, I investigated how those factors, such as religions and social norms, influence firms' responses to environmental and social challenges.
I also study emerging technologies such as blockchains and cryptocurrencies, examining the factors that shape the legitimacy of these technologies and how they are perceived by regulators, investors, and the public.
Feel free to reach out with any questions!

Research Interests

My research interests include nonmarket strategy, informal institutions, and emerging technologies. In the projects of nonmarket strategy, I explore how stakeholders and shareholders influenced by religious logic shape firms' risk-taking behaviors and environmental performance. In the projects of blockchain technology and cryptocurrencies, I examined the effects of news media and political shocks in shaping the legitimacy and trustworthiness of these technologies.
